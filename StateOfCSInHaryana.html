State of CS education in pilot states..

What we need to fix

Posted by Himanshu Thakur on January 12, 2018

Computer science education in UP and Bihar are on the two opposite ends of the spectrum. Where the highly expensive private schools have decent to good computer labs that are used at least twice a week, a government school mostly don't have a great computer lab and whatever they have is not utilised properly. Also the computer science education is mostly rote learning of programming languages and actually exploration based on interest of the child.A major problem is the number of kids attending schools.Very few children from the backward class of the society are able to attend private schools and those who do can't afford computers or any other extras because most of the family's earning go towards paying the fees. Rest of the kids who attend government primary and secondary schools very seldom have access to computers.

Most underprivileged kids in Haryana didn't have access to computers at home but were quite enthusiastic about learning. One by one teaching as a the method has proven to be quite helpful but the lack of practice nulls the whole effort and the enthusiasm of the kids. Access to cheap computers can solve this probelm and will truly give the kids an opportunity to achieve their dreams.
